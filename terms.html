4.Commission & Fees

Minyt charges Creators a commission on each sale. The commission rate is based on the Creator's total sales volume:

  • 10% on the portion of total sales from $0 to $50,000.
  • 8% on the portion of total sales from $50,000 to $100,000.
  • 6% on the portion of total sales above $100,000.

Commission rates apply to the sale amount only. Payment processing fees charged by our payment processor (Stripe) are separate from Minyt's commission and are the Creator's responsibility. Processing fees are generally not returned by the processor when a payment is refunded. Commission rates and fee structure may change; changes will be posted on this page and apply to sales made after the change.

Sales tax, VAT & GST

Depending on a Buyer's location, sales tax, VAT, GST, or similar transaction taxes may apply to a purchase. Where such tax applies, it is the responsibility of the Buyer, and it is calculated based on the Buyer's location and added to the purchase at checkout, so that the total a Buyer pays may be higher than the listed price of an offer.

Minyt's commission, and a Creator's earnings, are calculated on the pre-tax sale amount of an offer. Transaction tax collected at checkout is not part of a Creator's earnings or of Minyt's commission. Creators remain responsible for their own income taxes as described in Section 3.

Minyt's commission on refunds

If a refund is granted for a purchase made within 7 days of that purchase, Minyt will also refund its commission for that sale, so that the refund can be made whole apart from any non-refundable processing fees.

If a refund is granted more than 7 days after the purchase, Minyt's commission for that sale is non-refundable, and the cost of the refund is the Creator's sole responsibility.

In all cases, payment processing fees charged by Stripe are not returned and are the Creator's responsibility. "Full refund" therefore means the purchase amount less any non-refundable processing fees. Minyt facilitates refunds but does not itself guarantee, fund, or arbitrate them, except with respect to its own commission as described above.
